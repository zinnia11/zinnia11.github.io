Focal Flowers

Image from: Peony Pink Flower by Nowaja at https://pixabay.com/photos/peony-pink-peony-flower-4250969/, CC0. Peonies
These large blooms are very popular wedding and graduation flowers as they symbolize good luck and prosperity. In Chinese culture, they are known as the king of the flowers and are used for important holidays. In greek myths however, the peony has come to mean bashfulness, which is especially true for white peonies!
Since peonies are a very round flower, they work well individually. But in a bouquet, make sure to pair them with longer flowers like snapdragon.
Dahlias
Dahlias typically represent strength and grace, especially when under pressure. In the Victorian era, they were also used to symbolize commitment, or an everlasting bond.
Dahlias are a very big and heavy flower on their own, so they can function just fine without any other flowers in the bouquet. Just make sure to add some foliage to the bouquet.		 Image from: Pink Dahlias by Max Pixel at https://www.maxpixel.net/Flower-Dahlia-Garden-Bud-Dahlia-Dahlias-Bud-3587361, CC0.
Image from: Lilium candidum by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Lilium_candidum_1.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Lilies
They are a very popular flower and thus have commonly know meanings as well, like purity, beauty, and elegance. The lily is also a common flower seen at funerals, which is why they are also associated with grief.
Be careful to not overcrowd the bouquet, but lilies go well with other bigger flowers like roses. Also make sure to add some filler flowers like bay's breath to take up space.
Daisies
Daisies are a simple and common flower, and thus they often symbolize purity and innocence. This meaning stems from and old Celtic tale where God would sprinkle daisies across the earth for parents who had lost a child.
Daisies can go well with more colorful flowers and will benefit with a little bit of foliage to contrast.		 Image from: Daisy Flowers in the Field by rawpixel at https://www.rawpixel.com/image/3284373/free-photo-image-flowers-daisy-pictures, CC0 1.0.
Image from: Pink Carnation by Max Pixel at https://www.maxpixel.net/Pink-Carnation-Carnation-Flower-Carnations-3469748, CC0. Carnations
Carnations are known to symbolize devotion and motherly love, which is why they are a common gift to mother's everywhere on Mother's Day. Students at Oxford University will also wear carnations to their exams for luck. Learn more about this tradition here.
Carnations go well visually with simple roses, chrysanthemums, baby's breath, and eucalyptus.
Sunflowers
Most of the meanings behind the sunflower come from its namesake: the Sun! Common meanings are joy, loyalty, and longevity. This bright flower is perfect to gift anyone you want to wish eternal happiness to.
Use common garden flowers like tulips and zinnias to have a more earthy vibe to your bouquet. Or use a red rose to add some contrast!		 Image from: Sunflowers by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sunflowers_(44662222).jpeg, CC BY-SA 3.0.

Roses

Image from: Red Roses by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Red_roses.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Red Rose
The red rose is probably the flower meaning people are most familiar with. Red roses symbolize love, passion, courage, and romance. They are the typeical symbol of love given to romantic partners on special occasions.
Since rose have wide petals, they would look nice with flowers that have contrasting thinner petals like chrysanthemums and carnations. However, they also pair well with Calla Lilies
Pink Rose
Less common than their bright red brethren, pink roses have come to mean femininity, sweetness, and elegance.
Any type of small pink filler flower looks wonderful with pinkk roses, but for some color contrasts, try out lavender instead.		 Image from: Pink Roses Bunch by fi0101 at https://pixabay.com/photos/pink-roses-bunch-pink-roses-3941883/, CC0.
Image from: A Yellow Rose by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:A_Yellow_Rose.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Yellow Rose
Most commonly, the yellow rose means friendship, so this is a great flower to give to your closest friends! Otherwise, the birght yellow color makes people feel more cheerful, so yellow roses are symbolize joy.
With a bright yellow rose, just go all in with the yellow color! Add tulips, daffodils, or sunflowers for a monochrome yellow bouquet. Even with only yellow, make sure to have some foliage fot a nice contrasting green.
Purple Rose
Lavender roses, just like the color purple, invoke the feeling of mystery for a lot of people. This flower can also mean enchantment, splendor, and wonder.
Purple roses pair well with white flowers. Especially with white roses, you can combine their meanings to symbolice childlike wonder.		 Image from: Flowers Rose Romantic Purple in the Field by rawpixel at https://www.maxpixel.net/Flowers-Roses-Rose-Wallpaper-Romantic-Purple-3901824, CC0 1.0.
Image from: White Rose Green Leaves by saimouli at https://pixabay.com/photos/roses-white-rose-green-leaves-5747916/, CC0. White Rose
White roses are often used at weddings, and their main meaning, like other white flowers, is purity. They also represent youthfulness and innocence.
A simple white rose bouquet is the perfect bouquet for a minimalistic wedding. Pair them with some small colorful filler flowers for a bit more of visual pop.

Filler Flowers

Image from: Baby's Breath Flower by JanineAndersen at https://pixabay.com/photos/baby-s-breath-flower-bloom-2285053/, CC0. Baby's Breath
Baby's Breath is a very popular filler flower, and it is especially popular for wedding bouquets since it symbolizes everlasting love. Also, like its name, it represents the purity and innocence of children.
Lily of the Valley
Most often, lily of the valleys are viewed as a symbol of rebirth and humility. But it can also symbolize a return of happiness or even luck in love.		 Image from: Lily of the Valley by liz west at https://www.flickr.com/photos/calliope/153710989, CC BY 2.0.
Image from: Heather (Highlands) by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Heather_(Highlands).jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Heather
Heather flowers commoly symbolize good luck and admiration. The name of heather comes from an old Scottish tale where a woman was informed that her lover died in war with a heather flower. Thus giving this flower to someone can also represent a great love.
Statice
From Victorian times, the statice flower stands for memory and remembrance, thus it is used to tell someone "I miss you".		 Image from: Giant Statice by Bernard DUPONT at https://www.flickr.com/photos/berniedup/46251082975, CC BY-SA 2.0.
Image from: Daucus carota Queen Ann's Lace by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Daucus_carota_Queen_Anne%27s_lace.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Queen Ann's Lace
Sometimes referred to as the "bishop's flower", Queen Ann's Lace represents safety and refuge. There is a legend that goes Queen Ann was challenged to make a lace as beautiful as a flower and when she was making it, pricked her finger and that's why there is a dark spot in the center of the flower.

Foliage

Image from: Myrtle Common Culinary by WhiskerFlowers at https://pixabay.com/photos/myrtle-common-culinary-flower-4497949/, CC0. Myrtle
Myrtle has a greek origin and since then has come to mean good luck, love, and prosperity.
Leather Fern
Leather ferns have come to symbolize a lot of different things, like passion, productiveness, and feeling ready to do something.		 Image from: Leather Fern by Floral Inventory at https://www.flickr.com/photos/festivitiesmn/19184772015, CC BY-NC-SA 2.0
Image from: Senecio cineraria Silver Dust by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Senecio_cineraria_Silver_Dust_1zz.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0. Dusty Miller
Since it is just a foliage plant used in bouquets, there is nt usually much meaning given to it, but something that it has come to represent is sharpness of wit or industriousness of wit
Lemon Leaf
Lemons as a whole represent cleansing, since they have been related to water. Their flowers have come to mean longevity and healing. The leaves specifically really mean zest for love and life.		 Image from: Lemon Leaves Flowers by jammer699669 at https://pixabay.com/photos/lemon-leaves-flowers-plant-nature-1638200/, CC0.
