How to grow

This is a comprehensive guide on how to grow different flowers, including their difficulty and other important info.

Quick flower terms:
Perennial: Exists for many growing seasons. After the growing season ends, only the top of the plant dies. The roots live on and will sprout again for many years in the future.
Annual: Flowers that exist for only one growing season and performs their entire life cycle from seed to death in one season.

Beginner Flowers

Image from: Daffodil by Raw Pixel at https://www.rawpixel.com/image/6038840/photo-image-background-flower-public-domain, CC0 1.0.

Daffodil
These perennial flowers are very hardy and can grow in the mosjority of the Unite States. They can take both the cold northern temperatures and the hot southern states. Don't worry about care for these flowers, just plant the bulbs and you'll have beautiful yellow blooms for years to come.
Plant the bulbs in the fall before the first frost so that they have time to settle in the soil and then they will sprout up in the spring. Plant in a position where there is exposure to adequate sunlight. Light is one of the mos timportant aspects for good daffofdil growth, and these yellow flowers with glow even more in the sun.
This process is very similar amonst other bulbed plants like tulips.
Image from: Flowers Garden Marigold by Max Pixel at https://www.maxpixel.net/Flowers-Garden-Marigold-Bloom-French-Marigold-4695184, CC0.

Marigold
Marigolds are very hardy and don't need much care to thrive. They will continue to bloom all summer long no matter how much sun there is or how hot it gets. Short marigolds grow very quickly, so they can practically be planted at any time in the warmer seasons. However, there are some varieties that have a longer growing time, so doing some more research will be beneficial.
Marigolds bloom well in drier soil with a lot of sunlight. The soil needs to be semi-fertile though for marigolds to survive! If planted in a moist, shady area, marigolds are prone to powdery mildew and will likely die.
Image from: Ice Begonias Flower Garden by Hans at https://pixabay.com/photos/ice-begonias-flower-blossoms-pink-225909/, CC0.

Begonia
Begonias are a beautiful shrub plant that can be grown outside or inside as a houseplant. This plant is super easy to propagate! Just take a leaf or a stalk and put it in water until there are visible roots and then you can plant it in soil. Or honestly just keep it in water, making sure to change the water every few weeks.
Be careful not to overwater them, and remove any dried flowers or leaves, but other than that, you don't have to take care of this plant much. Just enjoy your beautiful small pink bunches!
Image from: Pansies by Piqsels at https://www.piqsels.com/en/public-domain-photo-zospd, CC0.

Pansies
These small flowers will for sure brighten up your starter flower garden! Pansies are surprisingly hardy towards cold and survive very well in colder climates. They actually act like perennial plants in cold plants, but most consdier them annuals, since they do need to be replanted in many warmer clmates.
Pansies are harder to start from seeds, so buying smaller baby plants is usually the easiest way to have pansies in your garden. When starting from seed, plant early in the string before the final frost to have the best chance of them flowering early.
Image from: Zinnias Garden Flower by jggrz at https://pixabay.com/photos/zinnias-garden-garden-flower-4397530/, CC0.

Zinnia
These garden flowers are one of the easiest to grow, and they look wonderful too. Their bright and full blooms are a wonderful addition to any garden. Zinnias also attract butterflies, so you'll also have some pretty visitors too!
Do not transplant zinnias, as they often struggle to thrive when moved from their birth soil. These flowers are also annual, so you must replant them every year. Plant them directly from the seed wherever you want them to grow later in the spring, after the frost. Full sun is very important for zinnia growth.

Intermediate Flowers

Image from: Flower Sunflower 2485 by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Flower-Sunflower-2485.jpg, CC BY-SA 3.0.

Sunflower
Sunflowers are tall and beautiful and a great yellow addition to a garden. They are also sun-seeking so they turn their heads to face the sun. However, they are a little more picky than other flowers.
They need a lot of sun, as their name might suggest. The soil they are planted in should not be compact as their roots need to extend deep into the ground. Since they grow so tall and big, their soil must be nutrient rich to support their growth. Finally, it would be nice to plant them in a wind sheltered area since they do have a tencdency of falling over. They are also an annual plant that needs to be replanted every year.

Image from: Irises - Flowers of the Holy Trinity by Lawrence OP at https://www.flickr.com/photos/paullew/26553531034, CC BY-NC-ND 2.0.

Iris
Irises are beautiful flowers, fittingly named after the Greek goddess who rode rainbows as they come in a variety of colors. These slightly harder to please flowers will look bright and beautiful if raised right
Irises have more specific soil and sunlight needs. the soild must be slightly acidic, which can be treated through additives to the soil. They must be in full sunlight to bloom and especially caonnot be covered by any other flowers, so they do well with their own special bed to grow in.
Sweet Pea Lathyrus Plant by YvonneHuijbens at https://pixabay.com/photos/sweet-pea-lathyrus-plant-summer-4313853/, CC0.

Sweet Pea
Sweet Pea flowers grow on a vine and will wrap around a balcony or a trellis if given one. They have very dainty flowers in a variety of colors and an enchanting fragrance, probably why so many scents use them.
Even if they look very delicate, they are hardy against the cold, and thus sowing early is a must to have them grow well. Thus they also do better in colder climates, and are very hard to grow in places with no frost. Their seeds are finicky to germinate, and don't worry if you just can't get them to grow for you, since there are many other climbing plants, like honeysuckle, that are much easier to grow.

Advanced Flowers

Image from: Rose Bush by Tony Alter at https://www.flickr.com/photos/78428166@N00/6958278774, CC BY 2.0.

Rose
Even though they are so beautiful and so popular for bouquets, roses are notoriously difficult flowers to grow. However, once you have a well established rose bush that you take good care of, they will flower year after year, and you will have beautiful self grow roses.
One of the reasons why they are so difficult to grow is because they are delicate when first planted and because they are prone to diseases. Thus, they need a minimum of 6 hours of direct sulight daily. Planting them in an area where the sun will hit them right in the morning is also iportant, as have the dew on their leave evaporate quickly is essential in not having diseases on the leaves of the bush. Make sure not to crowd the rose bushes, and plant potted roses in late spring, or just anytime in the growing season!
Image from: Purple Orchid Flower by Wikimedia Commons at https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Purple_Orchid_Flower.jpg, CC BY-SA 4.0.

Orchid
From seed to bloom, cultivating orchids used to take upwards of seven years! Now however, they can be easily mass produced as houseplants. Of course, once they die, they are incredibly hard to get to flower again and thus need a lot of tender loving care.
If you get a cheap potted orchid from any store, make sure to repot them so that there can be airflow around the roots! Root rot is one of the major problems orchids face. However, do not repot while the plant is flowering as the existing bloos may be damaged. Since the roots must have airflow, choose a porous soil to plant orchids in. If you orchid is growing wonderfully, yet is not blooming, it might be because there is not enough light. Move it to a sunny window so that it get adequate lighting. Another sign the plant needs more light is dark green leaves. The leaves should be a brighter green and that will show once you move the plant to sun. The roots whould be colored a bright white. If it is any other color, that is a sign that the orchid is not getting the correct amount of water. There are honestly so many important points to growing orchids, so here is an outside source that better outlines them: https://oregonorchidsociety.org/top-ten-orchid-growing-tips.
Gardenia White Dew Flower by Max Pixel at https://www.maxpixel.net/Gardenia-White-Dew-Flower-2064199, CC0.

Gardenias
Another flower that is difficult to grow well is the gardenia. These beautiful white and fragrant flowers will only come with a lot of hard wwork and extra growing care.
These plants are native to a tropical climate meaning that they prefer warmer climate(but not too much sun) and acidic soil. People who live in the northern states may be out of luck for growing gardenias, although they can also work as a houseplant. Gardenias should be planted in the fall at least six weeks before first frost. They also must be protected from any harsh winds in the winter. In the summer, make sure they are in a shady enough area to get sun the morning, but shade in the hours of midday when the sun is the strongest. Soil should be well fertilizer and draining. Gardenias do not transplant well, so make sure to be gentle with the roots and dig a wide and deep enough hole for the whole root system.
